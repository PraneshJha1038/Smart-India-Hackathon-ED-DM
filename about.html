Kashmir Earthquake 2005

Lives Lost
73,000+ lives lost
People Affected
3.5 million people affected

The deadliest earthquake in South Asia's recorded history struck at 8:50 AM on a Saturday morning. Most victims were trapped in collapsed buildings - concrete structures that became death traps. Children were in schools, families were having breakfast. In 90 seconds, entire communities disappeared.

The Lesson:

Most deaths occurred due to building collapses. Proper evacuation drills and earthquake-resistant construction knowledge could have saved tens of thousands of lives.

Commander of Kosi Flood Rescue

During the 2008 Kosi floods, NDRF commander Aniruddh Kumar led massive rescue operations, deploying boats and air support to save thousands. His leadership ensured safe evacuation of vulnerable groups, proving the importance of organized disaster response.

üìç Andhra Pradesh, 2015

Bravery at Bay of Bengal

Captain Radhika Menon braved violent seas to rescue seven stranded fishermen in the Bay of Bengal. Maneuvering her oil tanker through towering waves, she risked everything, becoming the first woman to win the IMO bravery award.
